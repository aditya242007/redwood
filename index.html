❖ Common Name: Toon / Red Cedar Tree

❖ Scientific Name: Toona ciliata

❖ Classification:

Kingdom: Plantae

Family: Meliaceae

Order: Sapindales

❖ Mode of Propagation:

Toona ciliata reproduces by seed. It is a prolific seed producer and establishes readily. Fresh seeds maximize recruitment success, and germination may take up to 12 months. Cuttings can also be propagated.

❖ Uses:

The flowers are used to create a sulfur-yellow color. The bark has historically been used to make twines and string bags. It is an important timber tree, providing valuable hardwood for furniture, ornamental paneling, shipbuilding, and musical instruments.

❖ Soil Needed:

  • Grows best in fertile, well-drained, loamy soils with a slightly acidic to neutral pH. It prefers moisture but is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

❖ Life Span:

  • Red cedar trees can live up to 100 years or more in optimal conditions.

❖ Some Diseases that Affect Tree Growth:

  • Common pests include shoot borers, aphids, and caterpillars.
  • Diseases: Susceptible to powdery mildew, leaf spots, and root rot if drainage is poor.

❖ Medical Uses:

  • Bark and leaves are used for their antibacterial properties.
  • Traditionally used as a liver tonic and for detoxification.
  • Contains compounds that may act as antioxidants.
  • Bark decoctions have been used to treat digestive issues.
